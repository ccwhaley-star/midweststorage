What this means — and what to do

  • We missed 173 calls in June, and 142 of them were during business hours — about 5 lost calls every working day. At a storage business, a missed call is often a rental that goes somewhere else. This is the biggest opportunity in the data.
  • Bourbon and Reno are where calls are slipping. Bourbon drops 46% of its calls (nearly half), and Reno loses the most in raw numbers (86). Labette, by contrast, only misses 14% — proof this is fixable with coverage, not a systemic problem. Action: add phone coverage or overflow routing for Bourbon and Reno during the 9 AM–4 PM window.
  • Staff the peak. Mondays and the 9 AM–4 PM window are the heaviest — that's where our best coverage should sit, and it's exactly where the misses are happening.
  • Block the Arizona spam. 108 Arizona texts hit the Reno line last month (all spam, zero real calls). Blocking that source cleans up the Reno inbox so real messages don't get buried.
  • Out-of-state demand is real. ~90 genuine inbound contacts came from movers relocating to Kansas (Oklahoma, Colorado, the Carolinas, etc.) — the same profile as several June rentals. Worth keeping our "new to the area" pitch sharp.
